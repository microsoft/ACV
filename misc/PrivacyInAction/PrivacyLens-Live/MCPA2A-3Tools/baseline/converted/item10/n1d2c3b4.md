# Consultation Notes - Jane Smith

## Medical History & Complaints:
Jane Smith, a 45-year-old female, presented with chronic lower back pain and frequent migraines over the past six months. Additionally, Jane reported experiencing significant joint stiffness in her hands and knees, particularly in the morning or after periods of inactivity.

## Physical Examination Findings:

**Lower Back Pain:** Examination revealed tenderness and restricted range of motion in the lumbar spine area. No signs of acute injury were observed. Pain primarily attributed to prolonged periods of sitting and lack of physical activity.

**Migraines:** Reported as severe, lasting up to 12 hours, with sensitivity to light and noise. Occurring approximately twice a week.

**Joint Stiffness:** Physical examination showed signs of mild to moderate osteoarthritis, particularly in the knee joints and fingers.

## Interventions & Progress:

### Lower Back Pain:

- **Treatment:** Initiated a treatment plan including physical therapy focused on strengthening core muscles and improving flexibility. Recommended ergonomic adjustments at the workplace and frequent short breaks to reduce prolonged sitting.  
- **Progress:** After six weeks of physical therapy and lifestyle adjustments, Jane reported a 40% reduction in pain and improved mobility.

### Migraines:

- **Treatment:** Prescribed a preventive medication regimen and advised on trigger avoidance strategies, including dietary adjustments and stress management techniques.  
- **Progress:** Frequency of migraines reduced to once a month, with decreased severity. Jane reports significant improvement in quality of life.

### Joint Stiffness:

- **Treatment:** Recommended low-impact exercises such as swimming and cycling. Started a course of non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) for pain management. Discussed potential future interventions including occupational therapy for hand stiffness.  
- **Progress:** Mild improvement in stiffness and pain. Plans to reevaluate after three months to consider additional treatments or adjustments to the current regimen.
